Abstract

Cotton is one of important oil crops, and the study that explores the genetic architecture of cottonseed traits from genome wide level and the influence of the cotton lint yield related traits on the genetic structure of cottonseed traits has important application value in the molecular improvement of cotton traits. Developing genetic analysis method for survival data is of great significance for exploring the genetic development of traits, mapping disease-resistance genes and the prevention and treatment of disease in human.

The phenotypes of protein content, oil content, five fatty acids, lint yield, boll number, boll weight and lint percentage were investigated and measured based on 316 tetraploid cotton accessions, which were planted in three environments (locations). Around 390K single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNP) were identified by sequencing all accessions. Based on full model mixed linear model, genome-wide association analysis was performed and a total of 124 significant quantitative trait SNPs (QTSs) were identified, consisting of 16, 21, 87 for protein content, oil content and five kinds of fatty acids, respectively. Genome-wide conditional analysis for the oil and protein contents, in which lint yield, boll number, boll weight, and lint percentage as covariates, respectively, was performed and a total of 129 QTSs were identified, consisting of 18 for protein content and oil content simultaneously. For oil content, a total of 70 QTSs were identified, and 39 of them were first detected; for protein content, a total of 78 QTSs were identified, and 52 QTSs were first identified. A new proposed family-based survival analysis method, which embodied recombination rate and association parameter in the genetic model, could not only effectively bypass the inflated false positive rate caused by population structure, but also unbiasedly estimate various genetic effect simultaneously, as well as various missing problems.

Cottonseed traits part laid the groundwork for breeding selection and synchronization improvement for cotton traits and family-based survival method part would provide effective support for survival analysis.
